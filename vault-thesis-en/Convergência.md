# Convergence

When a series, process, or system progressively approaches a stable value. The error decreases, the result stabilizes.

---

## Definition

In mathematics, a series converges if the sequence of its partial sums tends to a finite limit. In optimization, an algorithm converges when it reaches (or sufficiently approaches) a minimum of the objective function.

## In Neural Networks

The [[Loss Function]] converges during training when the error progressively decreases to a minimum. Each iteration of [[Backpropagation]] + [[Gradient Descent]] brings the weights closer to the optimal value.

Fast convergence: [[Relação Linear|linear relationships]] -- obvious pattern, few adjustments needed.
Slow/unstable convergence: [[Relação Não-Linear|non-linear relationships]] -- complex pattern, many adjustments, risk of [[Divergência]].

## In the Thesis

Convergence corresponds to [[Determinismo]]: rich [[Contexto]] -> low error -> predictable result. The system converges to the correct answer when the [[Ontologia]] is sufficiently complete.

"To the naked eye, at times it converges, at others it diverges" -- the observation about [[Vetor|non-linear vectors]] that captures exactly the training behavior.
