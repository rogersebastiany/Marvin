# Subset

A [[Set]] A is a subset of S (A ⊂ S) if every element of A also belongs to S. In the thesis, [[Context]] defines a subset of the [[Sample Space]] -- the region where the model operates safely.

---

## Formal Definition

A ⊂ S <=> for all x (x in A -> x in S)

Every subset is a restriction of the larger set. In [[Set Theory]], subsets are the fundamental operation of delimitation.

## In the Thesis

The prompt creates A ⊂ S (subset of the sample space). Each [[Tool]] creates T ⊂ S (additional subset). The intersection A ∩ T is an even smaller subset -- more precise, closer to [[Determinism]].

[[Bias]] in a [[Neural Network]] is formally a displacement that favors certain subsets of the activation space. [[Tool|Tools]] are subsets that function as bias.

## Unit Subset

When |A| = 1 -- the subset contains only one viable element -- the answer is [[Tautology|tautological]]. There is no probabilistic choice, there is [[Deduction]].

The goal of complete [[Ontology]] is to progressively reduce A until it approaches a unit subset for each decision of the model.
