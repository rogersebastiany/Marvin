# Complete Directed Graph

A graph where every pair of vertices has directed edges. Each node is reachable from any other node. It is "fully discoverable" -- tautologically.

---

## Definition

A complete directed graph (complete digraph) is a graph where between every pair of vertices there exists at least one directed edge in each direction.

If for all members of the set "nodes" the property "has a directional edge to every other node" holds, then that property is [[Tautology|tautological]] in that domain.

## Relationship with the Thesis

The concept of a complete directed graph was the starting point of the [[Ontological Tautology]] thesis. The original question: "if all nodes have directional edges to every other node, it is a fully discoverable directed graph, you know? By tautology."

The [[Ontology]] of the graph (definition of nodes, edges, direction) enables the [[Tautology|tautological]] conclusion that the graph is complete. The ontology is the cause, the tautology is the effect.

## Strongly Connected

A strongly connected graph is one where from any node you can reach any other node by following the edges. It is the property of being "fully discoverable" -- and when this holds for all nodes, it is tautological.

In the thesis: a system with complete [[Ontology]] is like a strongly connected graph -- every concept is reachable from any other, with no gaps.
